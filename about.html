About Us

Are you a business or organisation that wants to partner with us? Are you a student that wants to start your own hub? Email us at studentsagainstcorona@gmail.com!

We as students want to offer a helping hand wherever we can, during this coronavirus crisis as well as afterwards. Our organisation provides a strong platform to help individuals set up their own local hub and support them throughout. These hubs connect volunteers with those who need help, whoever they are depending on the needs of that community.

StudentsAgainstCorona stresses (inter)national hygiene guidelines to avoid spreading the virus between volunteers and, most importantly, to those at-risk individuals who we are helping. We urge all volunteers to thoroughly read the Recommended Hygiene Guidelines for Volunteers and watch the instructional video before performing any tasks.

If you are healthy, and at low risk, join this movement to help others in your community!

Sign Up!


IMPORTANT: Always follow your Government’s advice on hygiene measures. Inform yourself by reading the guidelines published by your national health service and keep in mind these are updated regularly!

We ask volunteers to also read the Recommended Hygiene Guidelines for Volunteers carefully and watch the ‘Volunteering Hygiene Video’ before you begin volunteering.



How can I help?

With universities having closed around the world, several full-time students now find themselves free. The main way you can help is by setting up a hub in your own community. The hubs look at the needs of their respective communities and try to fill that. Usually, a hub has one of the following set-ups:

  1. Personal connecting | On the ground helping of the vulnerable

    2. Examples: Loughborough, Oxford, Orange County

  2. Businesses | Helping restaurants and cafés

    3. Examples: Vienna, Berlin

  3. Strict lockdown | Helping from home and at scale

    4. Examples: Delhi, Pune

  4. Educational | Fighting misinformation

    5. Examples: Brikama, Kampala, Benin City

Signing up as a volunteer.

If there is already a hub in your community you can sign up as a volunteer. Tasks vary per community. When helping on the ground this can consist of going grocery shopping, walking dogs, chatting over the phone or perhaps even getting involved in operations. When helping businesses the goal is to provide a platform for customers to buy vouchers to their favourite places. In a strict lockdown, helping consists of connecting businesses and local governments or providing logistics to get things like food to those in need. Furthermore, you can help by raising awareness on facts about the coronavirus and hygiene.

How you can model your hub!

All Forefront Businesses Remotely Educational

Personal connecting | On the ground helping of the vulnerable

Examples: Loughborough, Oxford, Orange County

Main mission: Helping (self-)isolating individuals or vital workers, whilst recommending to adhere to and increasing awareness of (inter)national hygiene guidelines.

Forms of support Helping can be a wide range of tasks and differ per community, possible forms of support are:

  1. identifying people in need by advertising for your hub (e.g. posters, leafleting, digital newsletters, local press)
  2. grocery shopping
  3. medical prescription delivery
  4. dog walking
  5. facilitating free flower delivery
  6. sending hand written notes of encouragement
  7. organising outdoor activities (e.g. concerts or arts and crafts projects) near homes to provide entertainment and recreation for self-isolating seniors or children
  8. online tutoring
  9. collecting gifts bags or necessary items for vital workers or businesses
  10. distributing hand sanitizers and antibacterial wipes, mainly to volunteers
  11. collaborating with professional organisations to combine efforts or to provide professional care where needed (e.g. churches, food banks, elderly homes; e.g. professional call centres for chats on the phone)
  12. supporting organisations by sharing the recommended hygiene guidelines for their volunteers

Operations

Operations mainly consist of making a large impact on the community through a variety of small tasks. The main focus is to connect volunteers to people in need. The most successful hubs have been able to discover and help the individual needs of their community. This can be through volunteers performing tasks; finding help that you as an organisation can’t offer yourself; providing volunteers for events or services operated by other organisations; or sharing hygiene recommendations with other initiatives that do not advise their own volunteers on hygiene. In short, collaboration with local organisations, government or private, provides the most help to your community and success.

Likely partners

Existing charities that have already identified vulnerable people; local MPs or politicians that can direct you to area’s with a high number of unidentified people at risk; churches or local businesses that are willing to donate food, gift bags, flowers, cards, etc.; elderly homes that need support with daily operations or boosting morale (e.g. hand-written letters to those in isolation); local press (newspaper, radio, TV) is extremely helpful in advertising your hub.

Support from SAC

SAC can provide you with a brand, hygiene recommendations for volunteers, a network of people that have set up a hub in their community, PR material and guidance (logo, flyer, press release), a framework for operations, and funds. We are here to help as much as we can so if anything comes up you would like advice or support with you can always consult us.

Businesses | Helping restaurants and cafés

Examples: Vienna, Berlin

Helping businesses and individuals not covered by existing networks get the support they need whilst adhering to and increasing awareness of (inter)national hygiene guidelines.

Forms of support Helping can be a wide range of tasks and differ per community, possible forms of support are:

  1. identifying/contacting people and businesses in need
  2. helping businesses survive the time during which they have to close their doors by providing them with the cash that we will spend on them in the next months in any case through a voucher system through which customers can buy and use when these businesses are able to open their doors again (e.g. restaurants, bars, clubs, museums, theatres, shops, cosmetic shops, gyms)
  3. providing entertainment for elderly or children by organising outdoor balcony concerts or crafts and art projects for children
  4. supporting organisations by sharing the recommended hygiene guidelines for volunteers with them
  5. supporting organisations like www.helfenberlin.de by offering support for their operations

Operations

Operations consist of finding businesses that need help, seeing how you can offer that help, creating the online platform that offers the vouchers and bringing this to customers.

Likely partners

Local restaurants, bars, clubs, museums, theatres, shops, cosmetic shops, gyms that need help. Furthermore, existing organisations that are already helping people, but do not advise their volunteers on hygiene. And of course local press (newsletter, radio, TV) to get the word out about your hub.

Support from SAC

SAC can provide you with a brand, hygiene recommendations for volunteers, a network of people that have set up a hub in their community, PR material and guidance (logo, flyer, press release) and the back-end programming of the website, and funds. We are here to help as much as we can so if anything comes up you would like advice or support with you can always consult us.

Strict lockdown | Helping from home and at scale

Examples: Delhi, Pune

Main mission: Providing people in need with their basic needs (e.g. water, food) whilst adhering to and increasing awareness of (inter)national hygiene guidelines under the circumstances of a strict lockdown.

Forms of support Helping can be a wide range of tasks and differ per community, possible forms of support are:

  1. identifying/contacting businesses that can provide in basic needs (e.g. restaurants, delivery services)
  2. contacting governmental organisations in order to provide them your logistical services
  3. increasing awareness of the coronavirus and hygiene through digital channels
  4. brokering donations of food and other essential supplies between suppliers or manufacturers and distribution networks (e.g. government aid workers, authorised volunteers)
  5. organising curfew passes for key volunteers, e.g. food delivery associates

Operations

Operations will mostly be big impact projects (large numbers) and have to be done from home through digital media and consist of providing a logistics network/connecting suppliers with distribution channels. This means finding restaurants or other businesses that can provide basic needs. Connecting to governmental organisations or NGOs and providing them with your logistical services.

Likely partners

Restaurants, delivery services, pharmacies, PPE-suppliers, governmental organisations, NGOs, and national or local press (newsletter, radio, TV) to get the word out about your hub.

Support from SAC

SAC can provide you with a brand, hygiene recommendations for volunteers, a network of people that have set up a hub in their community, PR material and guidance (logo, flyer, press release), assist in acquiring key worker status so that volunteers can perform their tasks and the back-end programming of the website, and funds. We are here to help as much as we can so if anything comes up you would like advice or support with you can always consult us.

Educational | Fighting mis-information

Examples: Faisalabad, Lahore, Brikama, Kampala

Main mission: Increasing awareness of the coronavirus and (inter)national hygiene guidelines as well as fighting mis-information by spreading information made by internationally recognised organisations like the World Health Organisation (WHO). Other needs of the community can also be part of your awareness campaign.

Forms of support Helping can be a wide range of tasks and differ per community, possible forms of support are:

  1. increasing awareness of the coronavirus and hygiene or other community needs through digital channels or platforms like press conferences and schools.
  2. spreading posters and other information by making use of posters, videos and articles created by the WHO
  3. distributing PPE-material, like gloves, face masks, hand sanitiser and antibacterial wipes

Operations

Operations mainly consist of creating an online platform with a wide reach that increases awareness and fights misinformation about the coronavirus. Furthermore, it consists of hanging posters and reaching out to local and national press to contribute to increasing awareness.

Likely partners

Organisations willing to aid by offering their channels to spread information and national or local press (newsletter, radio, TV) to get the word out about your hub.

Support from SAC

SAC can provide you with a brand, hygiene recommendations for volunteers, a network of people that have set up a hub in their community, PR material and guidance (logo, flyer, press release), and funds. We are here to help as much as we can so if anything comes up you would like advice or support with you can always consult us.
